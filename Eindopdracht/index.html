Time

And putting it in perspective

What would the Earth's 4.5 billion year timeline look like, compressed into a normal day of 24 hours?

04:00 - The rise of the first simple, single-cell organisms, however, these organisms are destined to spend quite some time alone

13:00 - A simple cell engulfs another, creating a symbiotic relationship and the first eukaryotic cells, or cells with internal organs

18:30 - These cells begin to form colonies, and thus, the first multi-cellular life develops

20:30 - The first sea plants appear

20:50 - The first invertebrates, jellyfish, trilobytes, incredible diversification begins to take place

22:00 - Plants begin to appear on land, followed by land mammals

22:24 - The Earth is covered in carniforous forests, and the first winged insects appear

23:41 - Dinosaurs went extinct after replacing the many reptiles which died from a mass extinction event

And so begins the age of the mammals

In the last few minutes, apes split from the old world monkeys

And with only 1 minute and 17 seconds left before midnight, humans emerge

All of the recorded human history fits within a few seconds, an individual life lasting barely an instant, all a combination of 3.8 billion years of life

Did you know ...

  • The last known group of woolly mammoths survived until about 1650 BC.
    At that time the pyramids at Giza were already built for more than 1000 years
  • Cleopatra lived closer to our present time than to the construction of those pyramids
  • Oxford University is 200 years older than the Aztecs, 300 years older than Machu Picchu, and 150 years older than the Easter Island heads
  • If you're over 45, the world population has doubled in your lifetime
  • When Warner Brothers formed, the Ottoman Empire was still a thing
  • The jewelry company Tiffany & Co. has been around longer than Italy
  • When the dinosaurs were alive, there were 370 days in a year.
    The Earth's spin is getting slower because the moon's gravity is acting as a drag. Days are getting longer by about 1.7 milliseconds per century.
  • So in 140 million years, a day on Earth is expected to last 25 hours.
  • Because light takes time to reach us, everything we see is in the past.
    The sun you can see out of the window is 8 minutes and 20 seconds old.
    The light from our nearest star, Proxima Centauri, is 4 years old.

Time is precious, make it count!
